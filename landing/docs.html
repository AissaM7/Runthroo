Chapter 1

Capturing Pages

Runthroo captures a perfect, pixel-accurate copy of any web page — complete with all styling, fonts, images, and layout. You can capture pages one at a time or record entire multi-step workflows.

Single Page Capture

Use this when you want to capture one specific screen — a dashboard, a settings page, a landing page, or any single view.

  1. Open the page you want to capture in Google Chrome.
  2. Click the Runthroo extension icon in your browser toolbar (puzzle-piece icon → Runthroo).
  3. Click "Capture Current Page".
  4. The extension will flash briefly, then the page snapshot is sent directly to the Runthroo desktop app. You're done.
📸
Suggested screenshot The Chrome extension popup showing the "Capture Current Page" button

Recording Multi-Step Demos

Use recording mode when you want to walk through a full user journey — clicking through multiple pages, navigating menus, or stepping through a workflow. Runthroo will automatically capture each page as you navigate.

  1. Click the Runthroo extension icon and press "Start Recording".
  2. Navigate through your website normally. Visit each page, wait for it to fully load, then move on to the next.
  3. Runthroo captures each page automatically once it detects the content has settled (about 3 seconds of inactivity).
  4. When you've finished your walkthrough, click the extension icon again and press "Stop Recording".
Tip: Let each page fully load before navigating to the next one. Runthroo waits for the page to stabilize before capturing, so rushing between pages might result in incomplete snapshots.
📸

Chapter 2

The Capture Library

Every captured page appears in the Library — your central hub for all screenshots. Think of it as a gallery of raw materials you'll use to build demos.

Browsing Your Captures

Open the Runthroo desktop app and click Library in the top navigation bar. You'll see two sections:

  1. Platforms — Your captures are automatically grouped by the website or platform they came from. Each folder shows a thumbnail preview and the number of captures inside.
  2. All Captures — A flat view of every page you've ever captured, sorted by most recent. Each card shows a thumbnail, the page title, and when it was captured.
📸
Suggested screenshot The Library view showing platform folders and the "All Captures" grid with capture cards

Use the search bar at the top to quickly find captures by name. You can also delete any capture you no longer need by hovering over its card and clicking the delete option.

Chapter 3

Creating a Demo

Demos are interactive presentations built from your captured pages. You pick the pages, set the order, and define how viewers navigate through them.

Starting a New Demo

  1. Click "+ New Demo" in the Library view, or go to the Demos tab and click "Create Demo".
  2. Give your demo a name (you can always rename it later).
  3. You'll be taken to the Flow Editor where you can start adding pages.

Adding Pages to Your Demo

  1. In the Flow Editor, click "+ Add Page" in the timeline at the bottom of the screen.
  2. A panel will appear showing all of your available captures. Click on the one you want to add.
  3. The page gets added as the next step in your demo. Repeat to add more steps.
  4. To reorder steps, drag and drop the step thumbnails in the timeline.
📸

Chapter 4

The Flow Editor

The Flow Editor is your workspace for customizing each step of your demo. It has three main areas: a large canvas preview in the center, a timeline at the bottom, and the tools panel on the right.

Selecting a Step

Click any step in the timeline at the bottom of the screen to load it into the canvas. The tools panel on the right will update to show settings for the selected step — its name, transition style, and all available editing tools.

Step Settings

On the right panel you can:

  1. Name your step — Give each step a descriptive label (e.g., "Dashboard Overview", "Settings Page") to help you organize your flow and identify steps in branches.
  2. Choose a transition — Select how the viewer moves between steps: Instant (immediate switch), Slide (horizontal slide animation), or Fade (smooth crossfade).

Adding Click Zones

Click zones are the core interactive element in every demo. They tell your viewer exactly where to click to advance to the next step — a button, a menu item, a nav link, or any UI element.

  1. Select a step in the timeline, then click "+ Add Click Zone" in the right panel.
  2. Draw a rectangle over the area you want to be clickable by clicking and dragging on the canvas.
  3. The zone will appear as a subtle highlighted overlay. When viewers click inside it, they advance to the next step.
  4. If a viewer clicks the wrong area, a gentle pulse animation plays on the correct click zone — guiding them without being intrusive.
📸
Suggested screenshot A step with a visible click zone drawn over a button, showing the blue highlight overlay
Tip: You can add multiple click zones to a single step if there are several valid interaction points. Each zone can navigate to a different step (see Branching below).

Adding Branches

Branching lets you create non-linear demos where different click zones lead to different steps. This is perfect for showing alternate paths — like "What happens if the user clicks Settings vs. Profile?"

  1. Add a click zone to your step (see above).
  2. After drawing the zone, a popover appears. Under "Navigate to", choose which step this click zone should jump to.
  3. By default, click zones advance to the next step in order. Change the target to create a branch.
  4. You can have multiple click zones on one step, each leading to a different destination — creating a decision tree your viewers can explore.
📸
Suggested screenshot The click zone popover showing the "Navigate to" dropdown with available step names listed

Redacting Sensitive Information

Use blur zones to hide sensitive data like customer names, API keys, revenue numbers, or anything you don't want visible in the demo. The area gets covered with a frosted-glass blur effect.

  1. Select a step, then click "Add Blur Zone" in the right panel.
  2. Draw a rectangle over the sensitive area by clicking and dragging.
  3. The area will instantly be covered with a blur effect. You can resize or reposition it by dragging the edges.
  4. Add as many blur zones as you need — they're independent and can overlap.
Tip: Blur zones are exported into the final HTML, so sensitive data stays hidden even when the demo is shared externally.

Editing Text

Need to change a headline, swap out a customer name, or update placeholder text? Text editing lets you click on any text element in the captured page and change it directly.

  1. Click "Edit Text" in the right panel to enter text editing mode.
  2. Click on any text element in the captured page — a heading, a paragraph, a label.
  3. The text becomes editable. Type your replacement text.
  4. Click outside the text to commit the change. Click "Edit Text" again to exit editing mode.

Hiding Elements

Sometimes you want to completely remove a UI element from view — an ad banner, a debug toolbar, a notification popup, or anything that shouldn't appear in the demo.

  1. Click "Hide Elements" in the right panel to enter element picker mode.
  2. Hover over the page — elements will highlight as you move your cursor.
  3. Click on any element to hide it. It will instantly disappear from the preview.
  4. Click "Hide Elements" again to exit the mode. Hidden elements remain hidden in the exported demo.

Undoing Changes

Made a mistake? Every action in the editor can be undone. Click the Undo button in the top bar, or press ⌘ + Z. Undo works across all editing tools — click zones, blur zones, text edits, and hidden elements.

Chapter 5

Previewing & Sharing

Once you've finished editing, preview your demo exactly as your audience will experience it — then export a single HTML file to share anywhere.

Presentation Mode

Click the green "Present" button in the top bar to enter full-screen presentation mode. This is an exact preview of what your recipient will see when they open the exported file.

  1. Navigate through your demo by clicking the click zones you defined.
  2. Watch the transitions play between steps.
  3. Test any branches to make sure they lead to the correct steps.
  4. Press Esc to exit presentation mode and return to the editor.
📸
Suggested screenshot The presentation mode view showing a full-screen captured page with the subtle click zone indicator

Exporting Your Demo

Click the blue "Export" button in the top bar to generate your demo as a standalone HTML file.

  1. Choose a location on your computer to save the file.
  2. Runthroo generates a single, self-contained .html file — all images, styles, and interactivity are embedded inside.
  3. Share the file via email, Slack, Google Drive, Notion — or any way you'd share a regular document.
  4. Your recipient opens it in any browser. No install, no login, no internet connection required.
Tip: The exported file is completely offline-capable. It works even if the recipient has no internet access, making it ideal for sales demos in conference rooms, on planes, or in areas with poor connectivity.
