Why I Built a Free, Offline Demo Tool for Solutions Engineers

March 29, 2025

I'm a Solutions Engineer. My job is to show prospects how our product solves their problems — usually through live demos, proof-of-concept builds, and carefully tailored walkthroughs. A good demo can close a deal. A bad one can lose it.

For years, I relied on a mix of screen recordings, annotated screenshots, and live environments to build these demos. But every approach had the same problems: recordings go stale the moment the UI changes, live environments break at the worst possible time, and screenshots don't click.

The Problem with Existing Tools

When interactive demo platforms started gaining traction, I was excited. Tools like Navattic, Reprise, and Storylane promised exactly what I needed — click-through replicas of real products that prospects can explore on their own.

But the reality was frustrating:

  • Expensive subscriptions — most platforms start at $500/month and scale up from there, making it impossible for individual contributors or small teams to justify.
  • Cloud-locked — your demos live on someone else's servers. You can't use them offline, you can't send them as standalone files, and you're dependent on uptime you don't control.
  • Limited editing — want to blur out customer data? Change a name on screen? Add a branching path so the prospect can choose their own journey? Most tools either don't support it or lock it behind enterprise tiers.
  • Capture quality — many platforms use screenshots or browser automation that miss CSS details, custom fonts, or dynamic state. The demos end up looking "close enough" — which isn't good enough when you're trying to sell the quality of your product.

I wanted something different. Something that was free, ran entirely offline, let me edit anything on the page, and exported demos as simple files I could email to anyone.

So I Built Runthroo

Runthroo is an open-source macOS application paired with a Chrome extension. The extension captures any web page by serializing the actual DOM and CSS — not a screenshot, not a recording, but the real HTML structure with all the styling, fonts, and images preserved at full fidelity.

Once you've captured your pages, the desktop app lets you stitch them into interactive flows. You draw invisible click zones over buttons, links, and UI elements. When someone clicks the right spot, they advance to the next screen. Click the wrong spot, and a subtle animation guides them to where they should go.

But the features that really set it apart are the ones I couldn't find anywhere else:

  • Non-linear branching — build demos where prospects choose their own path. One click zone can go to the pricing page, another to the dashboard, another to settings. It's their journey.
  • Native data redaction — apply frosted-glass blur overlays directly in the editor to hide PII, dollar amounts, or proprietary data. No external image editor needed.
  • Inline text editing — change any visible text on any captured page. Swap "Acme Corp" for your prospect's company name. Turn $10,000 into $50,000. Personalize everything.
  • Single-file HTML export — your entire demo compiles into one .html file. All pages, all styling, all click logic, all transitions — embedded inline. No server. No dependencies. Just double-click and it opens in any browser.

Who This Is For

If you're a Solutions Engineer, Sales Engineer, or Account Executive who needs to put product demos in front of prospects — whether in a conference room with no WiFi, embedded in a follow-up email, or shared in a Slack thread — Runthroo was built for you.

It's also useful for Product Managers documenting new feature flows, Support teams creating guided walkthroughs, and Founders who want to showcase their product without giving access to a staging environment.

Free and Open Source Forever

Runthroo is completely free. No trials, no usage limits, no feature gates. The entire codebase is on GitHub — you can read every line, fork it, and contribute to it. It runs 100% offline on your machine. Your data never touches a server.

I built this because I needed it. I open-sourced it because I know other SEs and AEs need it too. If it saves you time, helps you close a deal, or just makes your life a little easier — that's the whole point.
